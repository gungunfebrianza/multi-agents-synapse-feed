Title: Dynamized Snapshottable Mini-Page Indexes

Thesis:
The right way to build high-performance mutable indexing is to make the data structure itself a concurrent mini-page index, make mutability snapshot-capable through reference substitution, and make the algorithm a dynamization policy that continuously chooses rebuild versus incremental maintenance under explicit query-cost constraints; without all three, the system loses either throughput, correctness under mutation, or cost control.

The System:
Start from the hardest constraint in **data structure**: a modern mutable index must survive concurrency, larger-than-memory operation, range scans, point lookups, and heavy writes at once. Bf-Tree demonstrates that this bar is achievable with a concurrent larger-than-memory mutable range index in Rust built from variable-length in-memory mini-pages managed by a circular-buffer pool; those mini-pages cache hot records, buffer recent updates, and cache range gaps, while growing or shrinking through a read-copy-update style reallocation/copy mechanism [PVLDB 2024 paper 'Bf-Tree: A Modern Read-Write-Optimized Concurrent Larger-Than-Memory Range Index' (vldb.org/pvldb/vol17/p3442-hao.pdf)]. Its reported outcomes set the structural constraint: 2.5× faster scans than RocksDB, 6× faster writes than a B-Tree, 2× faster point lookups than both B-Trees and LSM-Trees, 23.9× scaling at higher thread counts versus 14.5× for Leanstore, about a 75% cache ratio versus roughly 50% for comparison systems, 1.18 microsecond median read latency, and 70 microsecond 99th-percentile latency versus 131 microseconds for RocksDB [PVLDB 2024 paper 'Bf-Tree: A Modern Read-Write-Optimized Concurrent Larger-Than-Memory Range Index' (vldb.org/pvldb/vol17/p3442-hao.pdf)].

Impose that structural constraint onto **mutable**. Mutation can no longer mean ad hoc in-place state changes. It must preserve high concurrency and predictable correctness. Two supplied facts define the required form. First, Snapshottable Stores provides a mutable-reference abstraction for building persistent snapshots of arbitrary data structures by replacing mutable references with store references; it supports persistent snapshots and its core mechanism is formally proved correct [PACMPL/DOI entry 'Snapshottable Stores' (doi.org/10.1145/3674637)]. Second, Bf-Tree already performs internal mutation with read-copy-update style mini-page replacement [PVLDB 2024 paper 'Bf-Tree: A Modern Read-Write-Optimized Concurrent Larger-Than-Memory Range Index' (vldb.org/pvldb/vol17/p3442-hao.pdf)]. Combine them: every mini-page reference in the Bf-Tree-style index becomes a snapshottable store reference, so mini-page growth, shrinkage, and flushes remain copy-based while also yielding persistent snapshots of the live mutable index. This turns mutation from a risk into a mechanism: copy-based mini-page replacement supplies the mutation granularity, and store references supply snapshot persistence and proved correctness of the snapshot mechanism [PACMPL/DOI entry 'Snapshottable Stores' (doi.org/10.1145/3674637); PVLDB 2024 paper 'Bf-Tree: A Modern Read-Write-Optimized Concurrent Larger-Than-Memory Range Index' (vldb.org/pvldb/vol17/p3442-hao.pdf)].

Now **algorithm** must become the mediator that satisfies both the data-structure performance constraint and the mutability constraint. Competitive data-structure dynamization gives exactly the right algorithmic frame: it studies how to make static structures mutable under dynamic updates, models batch insertions and varying read/write ratios, and defines two targets—minimize total build cost plus cumulative query cost, or minimize build cost subject to a query-cost cap k [Author page for 2024 ACM Transactions on Algorithms paper 'Competitive data-structure dynamization' (cs.ucr.edu/~neal/Mathieu24Competitive)]. In this system, the algorithm is a dynamization controller over snapshottable mini-pages. It decides when to keep buffering writes in mutable mini-pages, when to rebuild a mini-page, and when to materialize a new persistent snapshot, using the dynamization objective to trade rebuild cost against query cost [Author page for 2024 ACM Transactions on Algorithms paper 'Competitive data-structure dynamization' (cs.ucr.edu/~neal/Mathieu24Competitive); PVLDB 2024 paper 'Bf-Tree: A Modern Read-Write-Optimized Concurrent Larger-Than-Memory Range Index' (vldb.org/pvldb/vol17/p3442-hao.pdf)]. This is structurally aligned with Bf-Tree’s actual update/query path—point lookups traverse the tree and search mini-pages, writes are buffered in mutable mini-pages before flushing, and range scans can use full-page-sized mini-pages for cross-node scans [PVLDB 2024 paper 'Bf-Tree: A Modern Read-Write-Optimized Concurrent Larger-Than-Memory Range Index' (vldb.org/pvldb/vol17/p3442-hao.pdf)].

The resulting idea is one system: a **snapshottable, dynamized, mutable mini-page range index**. The data structure is the mini-page tree. Mutability is the store-reference-based persistent snapshot discipline layered onto read-copy-update mini-page replacement. The algorithm is the online dynamization policy that controls rebuild, buffering, and snapshot timing under explicit query-cost bounds [PACMPL/DOI entry 'Snapshottable Stores' (doi.org/10.1145/3674637); Author page for 2024 ACM Transactions on Algorithms paper 'Competitive data-structure dynamization' (cs.ucr.edu/~neal/Mathieu24Competitive); PVLDB 2024 paper 'Bf-Tree: A Modern Read-Write-Optimized Concurrent Larger-Than-Memory Range Index' (vldb.org/pvldb/vol17/p3442-hao.pdf)].

Interdependence proof:
- Remove data structure: The system collapses into an abstract mutation policy with no proven high-performance substrate. The mini-page organization is what supplies hot-record caching, update buffering, range-gap caching, larger-than-memory operation, and the measured wins on scans, writes, lookups, scaling, cache ratio, and latency [PVLDB 2024 paper 'Bf-Tree: A Modern Read-Write-Optimized Concurrent Larger-Than-Memory Range Index' (vldb.org/pvldb/vol17/p3442-hao.pdf)]. Without the data structure, there is nothing for snapshots to snapshot and nothing for dynamization to schedule.
- Remove mutable: The system loses its central correctness and versioning capability. Snapshottable Stores explicitly enables persistent snapshots of arbitrary data structures by replacing mutable references with store references, with the mechanism formally proved correct [PACMPL/DOI entry 'Snapshottable Stores' (doi.org/10.1145/3674637)]. Without mutable state, there are no buffered updates, no mini-page growth or shrinkage cycle, and no need for the read-copy-update replacement that Bf-Tree uses to achieve its write and scan advantages [PVLDB 2024 paper 'Bf-Tree: A Modern Read-Write-Optimized Concurrent Larger-Than-Memory Range Index' (vldb.org/pvldb/vol17/p3442-hao.pdf)].
- Remove algorithm: The system becomes a fast mutable index with snapshots but no principled control over rebuild cost versus query cost. Competitive dynamization is the part that converts mutation pressure and read/write ratios into explicit decisions under two optimization goals [Author page for 2024 ACM Transactions on Algorithms paper 'Competitive data-structure dynamization' (cs.ucr.edu/~neal/Mathieu24Competitive)]. Without algorithmic dynamization, rebuilds and snapshot timing are policy guesses rather than cost-bounded operations.

Failure mode:
If the dynamization policy delays rebuilds too long, buffered mini-pages grow, query cost rises, and the system violates the query-cost objective even if the underlying index remains concurrent [Author page for 2024 ACM Transactions on Algorithms paper 'Competitive data-structure dynamization' (cs.ucr.edu/~neal/Mathieu24Competitive); PVLDB 2024 paper 'Bf-Tree: A Modern Read-Write-Optimized Concurrent Larger-Than-Memory Range Index' (vldb.org/pvldb/vol17/p3442-hao.pdf)].
If snapshots are not attached to the same copy-based mini-page references that mutation uses, persistence and live updates diverge, and the system loses the proved snapshot mechanism that justifies safe mutable versioning [PACMPL/DOI entry 'Snapshottable Stores' (doi.org/10.1145/3674637)].

Open question:
How should an online dynamization controller choose mini-page rebuild and snapshot points in a Bf-Tree-style mutable index so that persistent snapshots remain correct while total build cost plus cumulative query cost is minimized under changing read/write ratios [Author page for 2024 ACM Transactions on Algorithms paper 'Competitive data-structure dynamization' (cs.ucr.edu/~neal/Mathieu24Competitive); PACMPL/DOI entry 'Snapshottable Stores' (doi.org/10.1145/3674637); PVLDB 2024 paper 'Bf-Tree: A Modern Read-Write-Optimized Concurrent Larger-Than-Memory Range Index' (vldb.org/pvldb/vol17/p3442-hao.pdf)]?
